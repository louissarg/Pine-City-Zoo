GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the tallest living terrestrial animals and the largest ruminants. They are a species of African even-toed ungulate mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. Their long necks and legs, along with their distinctive coat patterns, make them easily recognizable.

Giraffes are herbivores, primarily feeding on leaves, fruits, and flowers of woody plants. They are known for their unique method of locomotion called "pacing," where both legs on one side move forward simultaneously.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?
